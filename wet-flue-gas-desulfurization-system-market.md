<p><p>Wet Flue Gas Desulfurization (WFGD) systems are technology solutions designed to remove sulfur dioxide (SO2) from industrial exhaust gases, primarily in fossil fuel power plants. These systems utilize a wet scrubbing process, where a liquid absorbent, typically a limestone or lime slurry, interacts with flue gases to capture SO2, converting it into a solid compound that can be safely disposed of or utilized.</p><p>The Wet Flue Gas Desulfurization System Market is expected to experience robust growth, driven by increasing environmental regulations mandating lower emissions and the rising global focus on sustainable energy practices. The market is anticipated to grow at a CAGR of 9.6% during the forecast period, supported by advancements in technology that enhance efficiency and reduce operational costs. </p><p>Recent trends indicate a shift towards integrating WFGD systems with other pollution control technologies, such as selective catalytic reduction (SCR) for nitrogen oxides, to meet stringent air quality standards. Additionally, the growing adoption of clean coal technologies and the retrofitting of existing power plants are further propelling market expansion. As industries transition to cleaner practices, the demand for effective flue gas desulfurization solutions continues to rise, indicating a promising outlook for the market.</p></p>
